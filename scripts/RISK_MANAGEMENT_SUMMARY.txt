================================================================================
KEY METRICS EXPLAINED
================================================================================

R-Multiple (Risk-Adjusted Return):
- Definition: P&L / Initial Risk (1R)
- Example: Enter $100, stop $95 (1R=$5), exit $105
  → R-multiple = $5 / $5 = 1.0R (1x return on risk)
- Target: Avg Win should be 2-3R minimum
- Importance: Objective measure independent of account size

Expectancy:
- Definition: (Win% × Avg Win in R) - (Loss% × Avg Loss in R)
- Example: 55% wins at 2.0R avg, 45% losses at 1.5R avg
  → Expectancy = (0.55 × 2.0) - (0.45 × 1.5) = 0.335R
- Interpretation: Average profit per trade in R-multiples
- Target: > 0.5R indicates profitable strategy

Win Rate:
- Definition: Number of winning trades / Total trades
- Target: 50%+ for typical momentum strategy
- Concern: <35% indicates strategy not working
- Note: Win rate doesn't define profitability (expectancy does)

Portfolio Heat:
- Definition: Total $ at risk across all positions
- Formula: Sum of (Position Size × Stop Loss Distance)
- Limit: 2% of portfolio (configurable)
- Purpose: Prevents catastrophic loss on single bad day

Drawdown:
- Definition: Peak-to-current portfolio decline
- Warning: >10% indicates significant adversity
- Critical: >20% triggers circuit breaker
- Recovery: Time to reach new peak after drawdown

VaR (Value at Risk):
- Definition: Worst expected loss at 95% confidence
- Example: VaR=3% means 5% chance of loss > 3%
- Use: Plan for tail risks, size hedges
- Limit: Monitor if approaching portfolio limits

Correlation:
- Definition: Relationship between two assets (-1 to +1)
- 0.85+: Positions too similar, lacks diversification
- Negative correlation: Valuable for hedging
- Monitor: Check correlation changes weekly
